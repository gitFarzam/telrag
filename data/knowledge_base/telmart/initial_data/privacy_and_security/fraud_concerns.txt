TelMart Fraud Alerts and Concerns

Identifying Deception and Hoaxes:
TelMart shares caution alerts at corporate.telmart.com/privacy-security to teach buyers regarding recognized scams and schemes not coming from TelMart.

Typical Deception and Hoax Categories:
- Phishing: Bogus communications or sites designed to resemble TelMart intended to grab personal or monetary details. When something appears too attractive, it's generally a phishing attempt. Stay away from uncertain hyperlinks, obtain downloads, or divulge confidential details over a call.
- Account Seizures: Scammers might unlawfully enter your TelMart profile to conduct unauthorized purchases, modify profile settings, or leverage stored monetary information.
- Identity Fraud: Fictitious profiles or dubious purchase trials may signal identity fraud.
- Fake Help Agents: Con artists might contact you claiming to be TelMart personnel. Ensure you're contacting TelMart by way of legitimate pathways.

Measures to Implement if You Fear Deception:
1. Rapidly modify your TelMart profile password.
2. Purge all saved monetary information from your profile.
3. Notify your financial organizations and request their fraud division.
4. Tell your financial card firms.
5. File a statement with your regional authorities.
6. Phone the Social Security Service and financial bureaus if you believe your character was stolen.
7. Monitor your credit records for questionable behaviors and enable fraud cautions or account blockers if necessary.
8. Tell TelMart Customer Care via the Chat with us option on TelMart.com about the scam.

Protecting Yourself Against Deception:
- Build powerful, one-of-a-kind passwords for your TelMart profile and every online profile.
- Engage two-factor security wherever you can.
- Stay away from public network systems when opening your profile or making transactions.
- Don't retain card info in your TelMart profile if you're troubled about security.
- Keep tabs on your profile and buying history frequently for any unforeseen actions.
- Alert TelMart Customer Care to any strange messages, phone calls, or actions.

Reporting Deception:
- TelMart Customer Care: Use the Chat with us option on TelMart.com or phone 1-400-840-4060.
- Federal Trade Commission (FTC): Head to reportfraud.ftc.gov to report deception or scams.
